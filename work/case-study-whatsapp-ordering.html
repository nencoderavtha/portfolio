Case Study: WhatsApp Ordering AI

Restaurant WhatsApp AI Agent.

A busy Hyderabad restaurant was losing orders every peak hour. We built and deployed their AI ordering agent the same day — verbal yes in the morning, live by dinner service.

Order Volume +45%
Monthly Revenue ₹2.4L+
Time to Live 1 Day

The Challenge

A busy restaurant in Hyderabad was losing orders every single night during peak dinner hours. Phone lines were jammed, staff couldn't keep up with WhatsApp order messages piling in alongside Zomato and Swiggy notifications, and the kitchen had no real-time visibility into what was actually in stock.

Regulars were giving up and ordering from a competitor down the street simply because nobody replied in time. The owner didn't need another delivery app — he needed someone to answer instantly, every time, without hiring three more people for the dinner rush.

The Solution

We built and deployed a custom WhatsApp AI ordering agent trained on the restaurant's live menu, pricing, and real-time stock levels. The agent takes the order conversationally, confirms it in seconds, processes payment directly into the restaurant's own Razorpay/UPI account, and pings the kitchen the moment it's placed — no missed tickets, no manual re-entry.

The owner keeps a live dashboard to mark items out of stock, and the AI adjusts what it offers customers instantly. Setup was same-day: verbal yes in the morning, agent live by dinner service.

The Financial Impact

Before the AI agent, the restaurant was capping out at roughly 40 WhatsApp orders a night simply because staff couldn't type fast enough during the rush — every order beyond that either went unanswered or walked to a competitor. With instant AI responses working around the clock, nightly WhatsApp order volume rose to 58, a 45% increase, without adding a single staff member.

Because orders route straight into the restaurant's own UPI account rather than through a 20–25% commission delivery app, the owner keeps significantly more of every rupee earned through this channel. On an average order value of ₹450, the added order volume alone puts roughly ₹2,43,000 in additional monthly revenue back on the table — commission-free.
